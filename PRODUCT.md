# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

PM0 is for founders and PMs working with AI coding agents in early-stage or founder-led teams. They carry product context across calls, tickets, analytics notes, repo history, stakeholder feedback, and memory. They need a lightweight way to make that context usable by the agent before code changes happen, without adopting a full product-management system.

Secondary users include product-minded engineers, operators, and AI-agent power users who want product judgment stored beside the code instead of buried in chat threads or disconnected documents.

## Product Purpose

PM0 is an open-source skill that gives coding agents repo-native product memory. It helps teams initialize `.pm0/`, analyze product surfaces, turn loose ideas into proposals, build small testable prototypes, and convert accepted work into engineering-ready PRDs.

The public site should help visitors understand the product philosophy, trust the workflow, and quickly see that PM0 is installable, inspectable, and useful as a small open-source tool rather than a hosted SaaS product.

## Brand Personality

Practical, opinionated, and open-source.

PM0 should feel like a sharp product operator's field notebook crossed with a command-line tool. The tone is direct, skeptical of process theater, and grounded in the actual mess of founder-led product work. It should feel useful before it feels polished.

## Anti-references

PM0 should not feel like generic AI SaaS, a heavy enterprise PM suite, a vague AI-hype landing page, or enterprise workflow software. Avoid waitlist framing, inflated platform claims, decorative dashboards, abstract productivity promises, and visuals that imply PM0 is a hosted product-management app.

It should also avoid feeling like a developer-only CLI toy. The page should stay accessible to founders and PMs while retaining open-source and repo-native credibility.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the product loop, not a vague AI promise.
2. Make the install command, source access, and open-source nature immediately visible.
3. Show scattered product inputs becoming structured product work.
4. Keep the copy concise, founder-readable, and skeptical of unnecessary process.
5. Preserve PM0's distinctive gravity-item hero while using it to explain the product, not decorate the page.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use semantic sections, keyboard-navigable controls, visible focus states, strong contrast, and responsive layouts that preserve the command, source links, and core explanation on small screens. Provide reduced-motion fallbacks for the gravity-item hero and any animated transitions.
